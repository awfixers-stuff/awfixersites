## Right of appeal

A soldier found guilty may appeal to the next superior authority within seven days of written findings. The appeal must state specific grounds: procedural error, new evidence, or disproportionate sanction.

## Review standard

The reviewing authority examines the record for fairness and proportionality. They may affirm, reduce sanctions, order a new hearing, or dismiss charges.

## Final authority

Appeals beyond [General](/ranks/general) are heard by the [Commander in Chief](/ranks/commander-in-chief). That decision is final within the militia.

## Record retention

All charge sheets, findings, and appeal decisions are retained as permanent unit records. They are not published except as required by law or lawful order.
