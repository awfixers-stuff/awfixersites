## Composition

A tribunal consists of three officers of rank not lower than [Sergeant](/ranks/sergeant), none subordinate to the accused in the chain of command. A [Captain](/ranks/captain) or higher presides.

## Hearing

- Charges are read. The accused may plead or contest.
- Evidence is presented. Witnesses may be questioned by the tribunal and the accused.
- The accused may present witnesses and written statements.

## Findings

The tribunal determines guilt or innocence on each charge by majority vote. Findings are recorded with reasoning sufficient for appeal review.

## Confidentiality

Tribunal proceedings are closed by default. Summaries may be released to the chain of command on a need-to-know basis.
