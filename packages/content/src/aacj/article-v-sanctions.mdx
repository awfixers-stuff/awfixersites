## Guiding principle

Sanctions exist to restore discipline, protect the unit, and remove soldiers who cannot serve the mission. They are not punishment for its own sake.

## Sanction table

| Classification | Typical sanctions                                                    |
| -------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Minor          | Written reprimand, restriction of duties, remedial training          |
| Major          | Reduction of responsibilities, suspension from operations, probation |
| Capital        | Dishonorable discharge, permanent bar from re-enlistment             |

## Dishonorable discharge

Reserved for major and capital findings where the soldier has demonstrated they cannot meet unit standards or have actively harmed the mission. Dishonorably discharged soldiers forfeit militia standing and may not sponsor recruits.

## Immediate removal

Where waiting for tribunal would endanger protected persons or operations, the chain of command may suspend a soldier pending hearing. Suspension is not a final sanction.
